Our Ethos

Podcast Club aims to embody these core values

Dialog

We believe that dialog is key to understanding. Whilst listening to expert speakers talk on interesting topics can be entertaining and informative, for us, the real value and laughing on top of those ideas provides further creative insight.

Synchronicity

All too often nowadays enables us to consume various forms of content whenever it pleases us. Binge watching an entire Netflix series in a night, for example. As a result, the extra sense of community that shared experience brings. 2. We value the extra sense of community that shared experience brings.

Discovery

The most rewarding experiences you can have will be out of your comfort zone. We believe that listening to an eclectic set of ideas that you wouldn't typically listen to is exactly the way you can expand your horizons.

Challenge

Be ready to have your opinion challenged and be on the front foot to (politely) challenge others. This is no echo chamber. We like structured arguments, as well as the willingness to alter them in light of someone else's insight are virtues to be honest and treasured.

Growth

Something we take on serious topics that really matter and can be difficult to discuss. We believe that having an environment where even controversial perspectives (held opinions can be discussed in good faith is essential for personal and societal growth.

Fun

Above all we believe conversation about fascinating topics is really fun! And we keep coming back because we can't get enough of it.

How it all began

Podcast Club was created by Martin O'Kane out of a desire for Conversation and Community

We've all had those conversations at parties or social events where you and a friend or new acquaintance discover you've recently read the same book, saw the same documentary or listened to the same podcast. You share ideas and insights and it's an incredibly rewarding experience.

Podcast club is an attempt to engineer those framed conversations on a regular basis, with the only overhead being listening to a podcast for an hour or two...
